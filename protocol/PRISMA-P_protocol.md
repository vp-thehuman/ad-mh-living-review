## Introduction

### 6. Rationale
Atopic dermatitis (AD) affects 7–10 % of adults globally and is associated with
chronic pruritus, sleep disruption, visible skin changes, social stigma, and a
substantial systemic inflammatory burden — all biologically and psychosocially
plausible drivers of mental-health morbidity. Cross-sectional surveys, registry
cohorts, and clinical samples have repeatedly reported elevated rates of
depression and anxiety in adults with AD, and several recent population-based
cohorts have raised concern about increased risk of suicidal ideation, attempt,
and completed suicide. Effect sizes vary widely across studies and reviews,
heterogeneity is high, and methodological quality is uneven (registry vs
self-report ascertainment, prevalent vs incident outcomes, varying confounder
adjustment). Existing reviews are rapidly outdated as new large-scale cohort
analyses publish each quarter — motivating a *living* synthesis.

## Methods

### 8. Eligibility criteria

**Population.** Adults ≥18 years with atopic dermatitis defined by
Hanifin-Rajka criteria, UK Working Party criteria, physician diagnosis
(ICD-10 L20), or validated self-report. Pediatric-only studies excluded.

**Exposure.** Presence of AD; severity captured as a moderator.

**Comparator.** Adults without AD (general-population, healthy-control, or
matched non-atopic comparators).

**Outcomes.**
- *Depression:* incident or prevalent, by validated instrument (PHQ-9, BDI,
  CES-D, HADS-D) or registry diagnosis (ICD-10 F32, F33).
- *Anxiety:* GAD-7, HADS-A, STAI, or registry (F40, F41).
- *Suicidality / self-harm:* ideation, attempt, completion, or self-harm
  presentation, by structured interview, validated scale (C-SSRS), or registry
  codes (X60–X84). Reported separately by outcome layer.

**Study designs.** Cohort (prospective, retrospective), case-control,
cross-sectional with explicit comparator. RCTs included for incidental
mental-health outcome reporting.

**Setting / region.** No restriction.

**Language.** No restriction; non-English records translated and verified.

**Time.** Database inception to most recent automated pipeline run.

### 9. Information sources
PubMed (MEDLINE), Embase (Ovid), PsycINFO (EBSCO), CINAHL, Cochrane CENTRAL,
Web of Science Core Collection. Trial registers: ClinicalTrials.gov, WHO
ICTRP. Grey literature: OpenGrey, ProQuest Dissertations & Theses, AAD / EADV
/ ISAD conference abstracts. Reference lists of all included studies and
recent narrative reviews hand-searched. Living-update pipeline re-runs
PubMed and Embase queries on the 1st of every month (UTC).

### 10. Search strategy
Full strategies in `search/pubmed_query.txt` and `search/embase_query.txt`,
peer-reviewed against PRESS 2015 by an information specialist (TBD). Strategy
combines AD MeSH/Emtree terms with depression / anxiety / suicide MeSH/Emtree
terms plus free-text synonyms and validated study-design filters. No date
filter.

### 11. Study records

**11a. Data management.** Records exported in RIS, deduplicated with
`revtools::find_duplicates()` and Rayyan, stored in
`data/raw_records_<run-date>.csv`.

**11b. Selection process.** Two reviewers screen independently in Rayyan
(blinded). Disagreements resolved by a third. Full-text screening identical.
PRISMA 2020 flow diagram auto-generated by `R/02_screen.R`.

**11c. Data collection process.** Two reviewers extract independently into
`data/extracted.csv` per `data/extraction_template.csv`. Discrepancies
reconciled by consensus; unresolved cases adjudicated by senior reviewer.

### 12. Data items
Bibliographic identifiers; country and study period; design; sample size;
mean age; % female; AD definition; AD severity distribution; comparator
definition; outcome definition and ascertainment; effect estimate (OR / RR /
HR / SMD / mean difference) with 95 % CI; covariates adjusted; risk-of-bias
domains; funding source.

### 13. Outcomes and prioritisation
Primary: pooled OR for depression, anxiety, and suicidality (each as a
separate outcome layer). Secondary: severity-stratified estimates,
sex-stratified estimates, continuous symptom-score SMD, quality-of-life
descriptive synthesis.

### 14. Risk of bias in individual studies
ROBINS-E for non-randomised studies; RoB 2 for any contributing RCT. Two
reviewers, independent. Domain-level and overall judgements stored in
`data/rob.csv`. Detailed plan in `protocol/risk_of_bias.md`.

### 15. Data synthesis

**15a.** Random-effects meta-analysis with REML τ² estimation and
Hartung-Knapp-Sidik-Jonkman CI adjustment, implemented via `metafor::rma()`
in R. Effect sizes harmonised to OR; HRs / RRs converted using study-level
baseline event rates with sensitivity restriction to natively reported ORs.
Heterogeneity reported via τ², I², and 95 % prediction intervals.

**15b.** When pooling is inappropriate (k < 3, or extreme heterogeneity with
non-overlapping designs), narrative synthesis following SWiM guidance.

**15c.** Pre-specified subgroup analyses: age stratum (18–39 / 40–64 / ≥65),
AD severity (mild / moderate / severe), sex, world region (proxy for
ethnicity / health-system context), outcome ascertainment (self-report scale
vs registry), and study design. Meta-regression for continuous moderators
(mean age, % female, mean AD duration) when ≥ 10 studies per outcome.

**15d.** Sensitivity analyses: leave-one-out (`metafor::leave1out`),
restriction to low risk-of-bias studies, restriction to studies with adjusted
estimates (≥ age + sex), restriction to incident outcomes for depression and
anxiety.

### 16. Meta-bias(es)
Funnel plot with Egger's regression test (when ≥ 10 studies per outcome) and
trim-and-fill as a sensitivity analysis. Selective-reporting bias assessed
within the ROBINS-E reporting domain.

### 17. Confidence in cumulative evidence
GRADE per outcome (depression, anxiety, suicidality). Domains: risk of bias,
inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, publication bias, and upgrading
factors (large effect, dose-response, plausible confounding away from
association). Summary-of-findings table generated programmatically.

## Living-review operations

The pipeline's living dimension is implemented through GitHub Actions
(`.github/workflows/monthly-update.yml`), which on a monthly cron:

1. Re-executes the PubMed and Embase queries.
2. Deduplicates against the existing corpus.
3. Opens an issue listing new candidate records for manual screening.
4. On merge of `data/extracted.csv` updates, re-runs the meta-analysis pipeline
   and re-renders the Quarto site.
5. Tags a release if any pooled effect's point estimate or CI changes
   meaningfully (pre-specified threshold: > 10 % change in OR or
   non-overlapping CI).

A formal annual re-publication (with revised PROSPERO record if eligibility
or methods change) consolidates all monthly updates.
