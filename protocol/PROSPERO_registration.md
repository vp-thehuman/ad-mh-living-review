## 1. Review title
A rapid living review with random-effects meta-analysis of the association
between atopic dermatitis and mental-health outcomes (depression, anxiety,
and suicidality / self-harm) in adults.

## 4. Anticipated completion date
First version: 2026-09-30. Living updates monthly, formal annual re-publication.

## 5. Stage of review
Preliminary searches: completed. Piloting selection: in progress

## 11. Review question (PECO)
- **Population:** adults ≥ 18 with AD by Hanifin-Rajka, UK Working Party,
  ICD-10 L20, or validated self-report.
- **Exposure:** presence of AD; severity captured as moderator.
- **Comparator:** adults without AD.
- **Outcomes:** depression, anxiety, suicidality / self-harm (each pooled
  separately) by validated scale or registry diagnosis.
- **Designs:** cohort, case-control, cross-sectional, RCT.

## 12. Searches
PubMed (MEDLINE), Embase (Ovid), Cochrane CENTRAL re-run monthly.
PsycINFO and grey literature limited to top 3 most-cited reviews
(rapid-review streamline). Reference-list hand-searches of all included
studies. No language or date limits.

## 14. Condition / domain
Atopic dermatitis (atopic eczema), ICD-10 L20.

## 16. Intervention(s) / exposure(s)
Atopic dermatitis (any severity, any duration). Severity recorded as moderator.

## 17. Comparator(s) / control
Adults without atopic dermatitis.

## 18. Main outcomes
(a) Pooled OR for depression. (b) Pooled OR for anxiety. (c) Pooled OR for
suicidality / self-harm (ideation, attempt, completion reported separately).

## 20. Data extraction (selection and coding) — RAPID-REVIEW DESIGN

This is a **rapid review** following Cochrane Rapid Reviews Methods Group
guidance (Garritty et al. 2021) and PRISMA-RR (Stevens et al. 2024). One
reviewer performs all screening, extraction, and risk-of-bias assessment,
with the following safeguards:

- **Calibration batch:** 50 records dual-screened by the reviewer against
  pre-specified eligibility criteria with a ≥ 14-day blind re-screen.
  Intra-rater Cohen's κ ≥ 0.8 required to proceed.
- **Title/abstract screen:** single reviewer; 20% of records re-screened
  after ≥ 14-day washout; intra-rater κ reported.
- **Full-text screen:** single reviewer; 10% sample re-screened after washout.
- **Extraction:** single reviewer; self-verification pass against the PDF
  for high-risk items on every included study after ≥ 7-day washout.
- **Risk of bias:** ROBINS-E (non-randomised) / RoB 2 (RCTs); 20% re-rated
  after washout.

Validation κ values are reported in the PRISMA flow diagram footnote of
the final manuscript.

## 21. Risk of bias
ROBINS-E for non-randomised studies of exposures; RoB 2 for any RCT.
Single reviewer. 20% of studies re-rated after a ≥ 14-day washout for
intra-rater reliability.

## 22. Strategy for data synthesis
Random-effects meta-analysis with REML τ² and HKSJ CIs; subgroups by age,
severity, sex, region, ascertainment, design; meta-regression for mean age
and % female (k ≥ 10); leave-one-out, influence diagnostics, low-RoB /
adjusted / incident / OR-native subset re-fits; Egger + trim-and-fill
(k ≥ 10); GRADE per outcome with one downgrade for rapid-review design
unless validation κ ≥ 0.8 (Garritty 2024 GRADE-rapid).

## 24. Type of review
**Rapid living review** with monthly automated updating and formal annual
re-publication.
