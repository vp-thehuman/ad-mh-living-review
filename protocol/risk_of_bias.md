# Risk-of-bias assessment plan

## Tools

| Study design | Tool | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Cohort, case-control, cross-sectional, registry-based | **ROBINS-E** (2024 update) | Higgins JPT et al. |
| Randomised trials (incidental MH outcomes only) | **RoB 2** | Sterne JAC et al., *BMJ* 2019 |

## ROBINS-E domains assessed

1. Confounding.
2. Measurement of the exposure (AD diagnosis).
3. Selection of participants into the study (or into the analysis).
4. Post-exposure interventions.
5. Missing data.
6. Measurement of the outcome (depression / anxiety / suicidality).
7. Selective reporting.

Each domain rated **Low / Some concerns / High / Very high** risk. Overall
judgement = the worst single-domain rating, with narrative justification.

## Process

- Two reviewers rate each study independently.
- Disagreements reconciled by discussion; persistent disagreement adjudicated
  by senior reviewer.
- Ratings stored long in `data/rob.csv` with one row per
  `study_id × outcome × domain`, plus an `overall` row per `study_id × outcome`.
- Inter-rater agreement quantified with Cohen's κ at the overall judgement
  level, reported in the final manuscript.

## Pre-specified judgement anchors (selected examples)

**Confounding domain (Low rating requires):** adjustment for at least age, sex,
socioeconomic status proxy (education or income), comorbid atopic disease
(asthma OR allergic rhinitis), and either smoking or BMI. Studies adjusting
only for age and sex → **Some concerns**. Unadjusted comparisons → **High**.

**Outcome measurement (Low rating requires):** registry-based ICD-10 diagnosis
applied uniformly across exposure groups; or validated scale administered
blinded to exposure status; or structured clinical interview. Self-administered
scales without blinding → **Some concerns**. Unvalidated outcome ascertainment
→ **High**.

**Selective reporting:** registered protocol available with all pre-specified
mental-health outcomes reported = **Low**. No registered protocol but all
likely outcomes (per Methods) reported in Results = **Some concerns**.
Missing outcomes plausibly measured (e.g., depression scale described in
Methods but no estimate reported) = **High**.

## Use in synthesis

- A **low-risk-of-bias subset analysis** is pre-specified for each pooled
  outcome (sensitivity).
- Risk-of-bias rating contributes one of the five GRADE downgrade decisions
  per outcome.
- Risk-of-bias summary figures generated by `R/03_extract.R` using the
  `robvis` package, rendered into `docs/results.qmd`.
